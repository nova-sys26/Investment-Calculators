What is Liquidation Price?

Liquidation price is the level at which your leveraged position is automatically closed by the exchange to prevent further losses. It happens when your margin is no longer sufficient to support your open position.

In simple terms, it is the price where you lose most or all of your collateral. Understanding your liquidation price is critical when trading with leverage because even small price movements can trigger liquidation.

How Liquidation Works

When you open a leveraged position, you are effectively borrowing funds to increase your exposure. This amplifies both potential profits and losses.

Liquidation Price = Entry Price × (1 − 1 / Leverage)
Estimated Move to Liquidation = 1 / Leverage × 100

Higher leverage moves your liquidation price closer to entry. Lower leverage gives you more room before liquidation.

  • Higher leverage → liquidation price moves closer to entry
  • Lower leverage → more room before liquidation
  • More margin → increases your safety buffer

Why Liquidation Price Matters

Many traders focus on profit targets but ignore liquidation levels. That is a critical mistake.

  • Liquidation can happen faster than expected
  • High leverage reduces your margin for error
  • One bad trade can wipe out a large part of your capital

Professional traders always know their liquidation price before entering a trade.

How to Use This Calculator

This tool helps you estimate your liquidation price based on your trade parameters. Before entering any leveraged position, you should:

  • Calculate your liquidation level
  • Compare it with your stop-loss
  • Ensure you have enough buffer

If your stop-loss is too close to your liquidation price, your position is extremely risky.

Professional Insight

Liquidation is not just a technical level — it is a risk management boundary.

Successful traders do not ask:

“How much can I make?”

They ask:

“How close am I to liquidation?”

Controlling this distance is one of the most important skills in leveraged trading.
