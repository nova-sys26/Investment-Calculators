Risk of Ruin Calculator

What is Risk of Ruin?

Risk of ruin is the probability that you will lose a significant portion of your trading capital over time. It is one of the most important metrics in trading because it focuses on survival rather than just profits.

Many traders focus only on win rate or potential returns, but even a profitable strategy can fail if the risk per trade is too high. This calculator helps you understand how your win rate, risk, and reward ratio interact over a series of trades.

How This Calculator Works

This tool uses a Monte Carlo simulation to model hundreds of possible trading outcomes. Instead of relying on a fixed formula, it simulates real trading conditions with wins and losses occurring randomly based on your inputs.

  • Win Rate – how often your trades are successful
  • Risk per Trade – how much of your capital you risk each trade
  • Reward Ratio – how much you gain compared to what you risk
  • Number of Trades – how long you stay in the market

The result shows how often your account reaches a critical drawdown level across all simulations.

Why Risk of Ruin Matters

Even with a winning strategy, poor risk management can destroy your account. A trader risking too much per trade may experience a losing streak that wipes out months of progress.

  • A high win rate does not guarantee survival
  • Risk per trade has the biggest impact on long-term outcomes
  • Small mistakes compound over time

Professional traders focus on minimizing risk of ruin before maximizing profit.

Trading Psychology and Risk

One of the biggest mistakes traders make is underestimating risk. After a few winning trades, it becomes tempting to increase position size and chase higher returns.

This is exactly where risk of ruin increases dramatically.

A disciplined trader understands that survival is the first goal. You cannot benefit from a profitable strategy if you are no longer in the game.

Consistency beats aggression. Managing risk is what separates professionals from gamblers.

This tool is not about predicting the future — it is about understanding the risk behind your strategy.
