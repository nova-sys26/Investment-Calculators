How to read the result

Funding is charged on the position notional, not on leverage. A 10x position does not pay 10x more funding than a 1x position of the same size. What leverage changes is the margin tied up, so funding can feel much heavier on your capital base.

If the annualized funding number is high, holding the position becomes expensive. If funding is negative, the position may earn carry instead of paying it.

What is Funding Rate?

Funding rate is a periodic payment mechanism used by perpetual futures contracts. It helps keep the perpetual price near the spot market by transferring value between long and short traders.

Funding Formula

Funding Payment per Interval = Notional × Funding Rate
Total Funding = Notional × Funding Rate × (Holding Hours ÷ Interval Hours)

For a long position, positive funding usually means you pay. For a short position, positive funding usually means you receive. Negative funding flips that relationship.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a positive funding rate mean?
A positive funding rate usually means longs pay shorts. It often happens when the market is crowded on the long side.
Does leverage affect funding cost?
Funding is charged on notional size, so leverage does not change the funding formula itself. It only changes how much margin you post.
Why is annualized funding useful?
It lets you compare a single funding rate to a yearly carry cost, which is much easier to evaluate against expected trade returns.
Can funding be negative?
Yes. Negative funding means the usual payment direction flips. In that case, the side that normally pays may receive funding instead.
