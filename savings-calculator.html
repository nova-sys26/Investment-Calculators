What is a Savings Calculator?

A savings calculator helps you estimate how much money you could have in the future based on your current savings, monthly contributions, expected interest rate, and time. It is useful for planning goals, building emergency funds, and understanding the power of compound growth.

How This Calculator Works

The calculator uses your current savings as the starting balance and adds your monthly contributions over time. It applies compound growth using the annual interest rate you enter, then shows the projected future balance and total interest earned.

Future Value = Current Savings × (1 + r)^n + Monthly Contribution × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r]

Where r is the monthly interest rate and n is the total number of months. If the interest rate is 0%, the calculator still works by using simple contribution growth.

Why Savings Growth Matters

Consistent saving is often more powerful than waiting to save a large amount later. Small deposits can grow into meaningful balances when they compound over time. This is why savings planning matters whether you are saving for emergencies, a home, retirement, or a major goal.
