What is CAGR?

CAGR means Compound Annual Growth Rate. It tells you the average annual growth rate of something over a period of time, assuming the growth had happened at a steady rate. That makes it useful for comparing investments, businesses, revenue streams, and portfolios that grew unevenly in reality.

The reason CAGR matters is simple: total return alone does not show speed. A 100% gain over 10 years is very different from a 100% gain over 2 years. CAGR turns that difference into one annualized number that is easier to compare.

Formula

CAGR = ((Final Value / Initial Value)^(1 / Years) - 1) × 100

The formula only works when the initial value is greater than zero and the number of years is greater than zero. If the final value is lower than the initial value, CAGR becomes negative, which correctly shows a loss on an annualized basis.

Why CAGR is better than simple return

Simple return tells you how much something changed overall. CAGR tells you how fast it grew per year. That is why CAGR is better for comparing different opportunities that ran for different lengths of time.

  • Use it to compare investments held for different durations
  • Use it to measure portfolio growth
  • Use it to analyze business revenue expansion
  • Use it to understand whether growth is actually strong or just slow over a long time
