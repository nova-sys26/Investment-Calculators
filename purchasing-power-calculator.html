What is this calculator?

This calculator shows how much an amount of money from one month is worth in another month after inflation. It is useful when you want to compare salaries, savings, rents, home prices, business costs, or any amount of money across time.

The result is the later month’s equivalent value needed to preserve the same buying power. That makes it much more practical than a plain inflation percentage.

How it works

The tool uses inflation data for the selected country and compares the start month and end month. It compounds the monthly inflation rates from the source dataset to estimate the later-date value needed to buy the same basket of goods and services. StatBureau documents monthly data via `get-data-json/jsonp`, and its inflation calculators accept month-based start and end dates. 1

Equivalent value = starting amount × compounded monthly inflation factor
Price increase needed = equivalent value − starting amount
Purchasing power remaining = starting amount ÷ equivalent value × 100

The line chart shows how the equivalent amount changes month by month. The second chart shows how much purchasing power remains relative to the starting month.

Why it matters

Inflation slowly erodes what money can buy. A number on paper can look the same while its real-world value drops hard over time.

Example:
₺1,000 in an earlier month may require several thousand lira in a later month to buy the same basket. The exact result depends on the country and the dates you choose.
