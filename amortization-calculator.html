What is Loan Amortization?

Loan amortization is the process of paying off a loan over time through fixed monthly payments. Each payment is split into two parts: interest and principal.

At the beginning of the loan, a larger portion of your payment goes toward interest. As time passes, more of your payment is applied to the principal balance.

This gradual shift is what makes amortization important—it shows how your debt actually decreases over time.

How Amortization Works

Every month, your payment follows a structured formula based on your loan amount, interest rate (APR), and loan term.

  • Interest Portion: Cost of borrowing money
  • Principal Portion: Amount that reduces your debt

Early payments mostly cover interest, while later payments reduce the loan balance much faster.

Amortization Formula

Monthly payment is calculated using:

Payment = Loan × (r × (1 + r)n) / ((1 + r)n − 1)

  • Loan = total loan amount
  • r = monthly interest rate (APR ÷ 12)
  • n = number of months

Example

Let’s say you borrow $10,000 with a 6% annual interest rate for 24 months.

  • Monthly Payment ≈ $443
  • Total Paid ≈ $10,632
  • Total Interest ≈ $632

In the first months, most of your payment goes toward interest. Toward the end, almost all of it goes toward reducing the principal.

Why Amortization Matters

  • Understand how much interest you actually pay
  • See how fast your debt decreases
  • Plan early repayments to save money
  • Compare different loan options

Without amortization, you only see the payment—but not the real cost.
