What is Amortization Calculator?

An amortization calculator is a financial tool that helps you understand how a loan is repaid over time. It breaks down each payment into two parts: principal (the original loan amount) and interest (the cost of borrowing). This allows you to see exactly how much of your payment goes toward reducing your debt versus paying interest. When you take out a loan, such as a mortgage or personal loan, your monthly payments remain constant, but the composition of those payments changes over time. In the early stages, a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest, while a smaller portion reduces the principal. As time passes, this gradually shifts, and more of each payment is applied to the principal. This calculator provides a clear amortization schedule, often displayed year by year or month by month. It also shows the remaining balance after each period, helping you track how quickly your debt decreases. Visual charts can further illustrate how interest and principal payments evolve throughout the life of the loan. Amortization calculators are widely used by borrowers, homebuyers, and financial planners to evaluate loan costs, compare different loan terms, and make informed financial decisions. They are especially useful for understanding the long-term impact of interest rates and repayment periods, and for planning strategies such as early repayment or refinancing.
