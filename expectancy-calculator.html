What is Expectancy?

Expectancy is one of the most important trading metrics because it tells you whether a strategy has a real edge over time. Instead of focusing only on win rate, expectancy combines how often you win with how much you win and how much you lose.

A strategy can have a low win rate and still be profitable if average wins are much larger than average losses. On the other hand, a strategy with a high win rate can still lose money if the losses are too big. This calculator helps you see that clearly.

Expectancy Formula

The formula for expectancy is:

Expectancy = (Win Rate × Average Win) − ((1 − Win Rate) × Average Loss)

This gives you the average amount you expect to make or lose per trade over a long series of trades.

Why Expectancy Matters

Expectancy is better than looking at a single trade because it measures your strategy over time. It helps you understand whether your trading plan is statistically favorable or whether it slowly leaks money.

  • Shows if a strategy has a real edge
  • Combines win rate and payoff size
  • Helps compare different strategies
  • Useful for both beginners and advanced traders

If your expectancy is positive, your system has long-term potential. If it is negative, the strategy may not be worth using, even if it occasionally produces winning trades.

Break-even Win Rate

The break-even win rate is the win rate you need in order to stop losing money over time. It depends on your average win and average loss.

Break-even Win Rate = Average Loss / (Average Win + Average Loss)

If your actual win rate is higher than the break-even level, your expectancy becomes positive.
