What is BTC Dominance?

BTC dominance is Bitcoin’s share of the total crypto market cap. When that share rises, Bitcoin is taking a larger portion of the market. When it falls, capital is usually spreading more into altcoins and other crypto assets.

The number matters because it gives a fast read on market structure. It does not tell you what price will do next, but it does tell you who is currently leading the market.

How this page works

The page loads current market data from CoinGecko for the dashboard cards, while the chart itself is a real historical BTC.D chart from TradingView. That removes the need for localStorage history and gives you a proper charting experience.

BTC Dominance = Bitcoin Market Cap ÷ Total Crypto Market Cap

Why it matters

BTC dominance is useful for understanding whether the market is concentrating into Bitcoin or rotating toward broader crypto risk. That makes it a better context tool than a simple price widget.

A higher dominance reading often points to a more defensive market structure. A lower reading often points to broader risk appetite and stronger altcoin participation.

How to use it

  • Check the current BTC dominance reading first
  • Compare it with the market cap cards below the gauge
  • Use the TradingView chart for real historical structure
  • Use it as context before making crypto allocation decisions
