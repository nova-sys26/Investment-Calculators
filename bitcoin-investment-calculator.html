Why this format gets shared

People share simple “what if” outcomes because they are instantly readable. The big number lands first, the date gives context, and the chart makes the story feel real.

Best-performing formats are usually short, specific, and visual: “If you invested $100 in Bitcoin on [date], you would have $X today.”
The page keeps the answer first, then gives the chart, then the explanation. That ordering is what makes it feel like a real product instead of a toy.
